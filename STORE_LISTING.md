## Detailed description (Chrome Web Store long description)

**Stop doom-scrolling LinkedIn. Keep everything you actually use it for.**

LinkedIn Focus quietly replaces the endless home feed with a calm, distraction-free screen the moment you land on it — no feed content ever loads behind it, so there's nothing to half-read, autoplay, or get pulled into.

Everything else on LinkedIn works exactly as before:
- 💬 **Messages** — fully usable, one click away
- 💼 **Jobs** — fully usable, one click away
- 🔍 Profiles, search, notifications, and posting — untouched

**How it works**
- The moment you open your feed, it's replaced with a focus screen and a short quote about attention and discipline.
- A **save streak** tracks how many times today (and this week) you've caught yourself and closed the feed — so you can watch the habit build.
- Genuinely need the feed for something specific? A single button unlocks it for **5 minutes**, then it locks again automatically. No settings menu, no nagging, no guilt trip — just friction where you need it and none where you don't.

**Why it's different**
- No tracking, no accounts, no network requests. Everything (streaks, unlock timer) is stored locally in your browser.
- Doesn't touch or hide any other part of LinkedIn — this isn't a blunt site blocker, it's a scalpel aimed at exactly one page.
- Built for people who need LinkedIn for messaging and job hunting but don't want to hand over their attention to the feed algorithm.

**Permissions**
LinkedIn Focus only runs on linkedin.com, injects no analytics, and makes no external network calls.

## Icon / visual identity
- Primary motif: 🎯 target — already used in the block screen, reinforces "focus"
- Color palette: deep teal-to-indigo gradient (`#0f2027 → #203a43 → #2c5364 → #1a2980 → #26d0ce`) — calm, not alarming; distinct from LinkedIn's blue so it reads as "a different mode," not a LinkedIn feature
- Tone: calm and encouraging, not punitive — copy says "Feed blocked," not "You failed"
